Life of Oliver "Tuku" Mtukudzi

Personal Details

  • Name: Oliver "Tuku" Mtukudzi
  • Born: 22nd September, 1952
  • Origin: Zimbabwe

Tuku's Bio

1952: Birth
Born in Highfield, Salisbury(modern day Harare)in Southern Rhodesia as the eldest of six siblings
1975: Debut Single Release
At the age of 23, he released his first single, 'Stop after Orange'.
1977: Wagon Wheels
He started performing with the Wagon Wheels band which featured popular Musician Thomas Mapfumo. His tenure with the band was short lived and he left the following year.
1978: Black Spirits
He left the Wagon Wheels band and formed his own group Black Spirits.
1979: Marriage
Tuku married his first wife, Melody Murape. the couple was blessed with two daughters, Sandra and Selmor
1980-1989: Music Life
Having established himself in the '70s, Tuku went ahead to release a string of successful albums which included; Africa (1980), Shanje(1981), Gona(1986), among many others.
1990-1999: Film Life
In the '90s he branched off to acting, starring in his country's first two nationally made films: jit(1990) and Neria (1992).
2000: International acknowledgement
Tuku made a breakthrough into the international entertainment scene. His album Africa(1980) was re-released and he gained much deserved global acknowledgement.
2000s & 2010s: World Music Circuit
During the 2000s and 2010s, he became a regular in the World Music Circuit touring Europe and North America. He was also very much involved in philanthropic work and Human Rights Activism.

Achievements and Contributions

Pakare Paye Arts Center(2003)
In 2003, Oliver Mtukudzi founded Pakare Paye Arts Centre providing a performance platform for developing and fostering young talent in many practical artistic endeavours particularly music, dance, drama, poetry and storytelling
UNICEF Goodwill Ambasador (2011)
For his continued advocacy for human rights and philanthropic acts,in 2011 Oliver Mtukudzi was named the Goodwill Ambasador for Eastern and Southern Africa

FootNote

Oliver "Tuku" Mtukudzi received so many awards and made many life changing contribution to the shape and view of music, film and art not only in Zimbabwe but in the entire Africa. He released numerous record hit albums and featured in different films. A father of five (5), two of his children, Selmor and Sam followed up in his footset and became musicians.

23rd January 2019: Promotion to Glory
On 23 January 2019, Mtukudzi died at the age of 66 at Avenues Clinic in Harare, Zimbabwe after a long battle with diabetes mellitus
